Solar Tracking Power Device

Senior Design Project

For my Senior Design Project, my team of four engineers created a device that maximizes solar energy input using machine learning, motors, and photoresistors to accurately align the solar panel of the device towards the sun regardless of the season. Our team has designed and fabricated a working prototype that can track a light source and has laid the groundwork for the machine learning aspect of the device that will predict the optimal angle to achieve maximum input.

In conjunction with a soldered PCB, the aforementioned photoresistors are placed along the four corners of the PCB to maximize the range at which light is measured. An analog-to-digital converter then converts the analog values of light input from the photoresistors to a digital input that could be used with the software to move the servo towards the direction of the most light. A Raspberry Pi 3 and a breadboard was used for the pin and wire connections.

To code the tracking of the device, we took averages of different combinations of the four photoresistors and compared them. If the top left and right photoresistors had a larger average value than the bottom left and right ones, the vertical servo moves the head of the device up and vice versa. If the top left and bottom left photoresistors have a larger average value than the top right and bottom right ones, the horizontal servo moves the head to the left and vice versa. A simple linear regression model to predict the position of the solar tracker. The independent variable is the time of day and the dependent variable is the position. We create a separate model for each servo and based on the models, we initialize the servos to that position.
